What is The Pirate Bay?

The Pirate Bay (TPB) is one of the world's most popular and resilient torrent websites, founded in 2003 in Sweden. Despite numerous attempts to shut it down, The Pirate Bay continues to operate through various domain names and proxy servers. It hosts millions of torrent files and magnet links, allowing users to share and download movies, TV shows, music, software, games, and more through peer-to-peer file sharing.

Why is The Pirate Bay Blocked?

Many Internet Service Providers (ISPs) and governments have blocked access to The Pirate Bay due to copyright infringement concerns. Countries including the UK, Australia, France, Germany, India, and many others have implemented blocks at the ISP level. However, these blocks can be easily bypassed using proxy sites, VPNs, or by changing your DNS settings.

How Do PirateBay Proxies Work?

PirateBay proxy sites act as intermediaries between your device and The Pirate Bay's servers. When you access a proxy, it fetches the content from The Pirate Bay and displays it to you, effectively bypassing your ISP's block.
